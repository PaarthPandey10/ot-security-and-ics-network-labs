# 2.3 – Infrastructure Build Guide: DHCP Infrastructure Automation

> A visual narrative documenting the transition from static addressing to automated lease management, featuring discrete network pools and infrastructure air-gap validation.

---

## Overview

This guide details the activation and configuration of the Cisco SF300’s DHCP server daemon to automate IP lease acquisition for VLAN 10 (Home) and VLAN 20 (Work). It covers the deployment of distinct broadcast domains, the implementation of static IP exclusions to protect core routing SVIs, and the validation of successful client migration and Layer 3 air-gapping.

---

## Workflow Steps

### 1. Act I: DHCP Server Daemon Activation
To transition the environment from manual static addressing to automated lease management, we first needed to initialize the core DHCP engine on the Cisco switch.

**Service Activation:**
![](../../screenshots/dhcp_Page_01.png)
*Navigating to `IP Configuration > DHCP Server > Properties`, the global DHCP Server Status daemon was enabled on the Cisco ASIC, allowing it to begin processing broadcast DHCPDISCOVER requests.*

### 2. Act II: Infrastructure Protection (Address Exclusions)
Before generating the network pools, it is a critical engineering practice to exclude IPs reserved for core infrastructure. Failing to do so would allow the DHCP server to assign IPs belonging to our Switch Virtual Interfaces (SVIs), causing catastrophic IP conflicts and control plane lockouts.

**Static Address Exclusions:**
![](../../screenshots/dhcp_Page_02.png)
*We applied strict exclusion ranges (`192.168.10.1` - `192.168.10.20` and `192.168.20.1` - `192.168.20.20`). This protects the `.1` gateways and leaves a block of 20 IPs available for future static infrastructure provisioning.*

### 3. Act III: Scope Provisioning
With the infrastructure protected, we instantiated two discrete network pools to ensure our broadcast domains remain logically separated. 

**Instantiating HOME-POOL (VLAN 10):**
![](../../screenshots/dhcp_Page_03.png)
*The `home-pool` was configured for the `192.168.10.0/24` subnet. We defined the default router as the local SVI (`192.168.10.1`) and pushed `8.8.8.8` as the DNS server.*

**Instantiating WORK-POOL (VLAN 20):**
![](../../screenshots/dhcp_Page_04.png)
*The `work-pool` was configured symmetrically for the `192.168.20.0/24` subnet, pointing to the `192.168.20.1` gateway.*

**Network Pools Verification:**
![](../../screenshots/dhcp_Page_05.png)
*The Network Pool Table confirms both scopes are active, bound to their respective subnets, and ready to distribute leases with infinite duration.*

### 4. Act IV: Client Migration & Air-Gap Verification
The final phase required migrating the physical endpoints (DP and WL) from hardcoded static IPs to dynamic lease acquisition, shifting the network configuration burden to the switch.

**Host OS Adapter Migration:**
![](../../screenshots/dhcp_Page_06.png)
*On the Windows endpoint, the IPv4 properties were shifted from manual configuration to "Obtain an IP address automatically" and "Obtain DNS server address automatically."*

**Lease Acquisition and State Validation:**
![](../../screenshots/dhcp_Page_07.png)
*Executing `ipconfig` in Windows PowerShell confirms the successful transition. The DP interface seamlessly acquired `192.168.10.21` (the first available IP after our 1-20 exclusion block), along with the correct `192.168.10.1` default gateway.*

```cmd
Ethernet adapter Ethernet:

   Connection-specific DNS Suffix  . :
   Link-local IPv6 Address . . . . . : fe80::b761:614d:a293:fad5%20
   IPv4 Address. . . . . . . . . . . : 192.168.10.21
   Subnet Mask . . . . . . . . . . . : 255.255.255.0
   Default Gateway . . . . . . . . . : 192.168.10.1
```

*Note: Subsequent ping tests to the assigned DNS server (`8.8.8.8`) returned a "Destination net unreachable" error. This is a **successful validation** of our network containment strategy, proving the lab environment is strictly air-gapped from the external production/internet routing table.*

## Technical Specifications

* **Core L3 Engine:** Cisco SF300-24P 
* **DHCP Pools:** `home-pool` (VLAN 10) | `work-pool` (VLAN 20)
* **Exclusion Ranges:** `192.168.10.1-20` | `192.168.20.1-20`
* **DNS Assignment:** `8.8.8.8` (Air-Gapped)
